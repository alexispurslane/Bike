C-like Syntax

Why cope with a new syntax, when there is a perfectly good family of syntaxes that will work perfectly well? C's syntax, although slightly verbose at times, has survived the test of time, and it works well, with only a few semantic changes for functional programming.

A Fresh, Clean Blend of OOP and FP

We've all heard this before: Oh, we have a great blend of OOP and FP in our language! But then, when we try it out, it's a spaghettified mess and a total disapointment, or a OOP language that has a map and a filter and practically nothing else. Other times, it doesn't even support any form of FP, but instead Generics programming of procedural programming. Not so in Bike. In Bike, we have taken special precaustions to preserve a clear functional heritige, while adding a clearly defined and separated OOP layer that integrates well with it's functional side.
